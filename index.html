Are Films Effective in the Classroom?



In this study, researchers used an interview style process to determine how much and what kind of information children can remember from educational films. These interviews consisted of six parts: a free recall, open-ended questions, recognition questions, important information questions, unimportant information questions, and personal experience questions. In order to compare, the control group of students was taught a school lesson, another group watched a film only once, another group watched the film twice, and the final group of students was taught a school lesson and watched the film.
